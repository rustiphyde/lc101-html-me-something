PAREIDOLIA

What The Heck Is Pareidolia?

According to the Merriam-Webster Dictionary, "Pareidolia" is the tendency to perceive a specific, often meaningful image in a random or ambuiguous visual pattern. I'm sure at some point in your life you've seen a cloud that reminded you of something else — whether that was a turtle, a dragon or Homer Simpson, it's all pareidolia!

Take a look at the picture above this paragraph. It is simply a tree located in Jefferson Barracks Park. Apart from having an oddly placed set of stairs behind it, do you see anything interesting about this tree? Does it remind you of anything? Does it seem out of place in this temperate climate?

What the Rorschach is Going On Here?

If you thought you were seeing a specific animal in the shape of the tree above, you're not alone. The mind works in mysterious ways and it's able to find patterns in just about anything.

Seeing Faces Everywhere

I have had pareidolia for as long as I can remember. I can see faces in the most random objects. Look around you. Does anything look like a face? An animal? A famous character or religious figure? If so you have pareidolia too. Don't be sad. You are not alone in the world. There are countless groups on Facebook dedicated to seeing faces in the weirdest places — such as this one: Things With Faces. So take some pictures of what you see and share them with world.
